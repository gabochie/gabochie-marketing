Google Maps vs Social Media:
Which Drives More Sales?

The honest, data-driven comparison for Ghanaian businesses trying to decide where to invest their marketing budget.

Published: May 10, 2026 | 7 min read

"Should I focus on Google Maps or Facebook/Instagram?" It's the most common question we hear from Ghanaian business owners. Here's the data-driven answer.

The Real Question: Intent vs. Interruption

The fundamental difference between these channels comes down to user intent:

  • Google Maps: Users are actively searching for what you sell. High intent = higher conversion.
  • Social Media: Users are scrolling for entertainment. You're interrupting them. Lower intent = lower conversion.

Head-to-Head Comparison

Factor Google Maps Social Media
User Intent High (actively searching) Low (browsing/entertainment)
Cost to Start Free Free (but ads cost money)
Time Investment 2-3 hours/week 10-15 hours/week
Conversion Rate 15-25% 1-3%
Results Timeline 2-4 weeks 3-6 months
Longevity Compounds over time Disappears when you stop posting
Audience Reach Local customers only Can target nationally
Content Creation Minimal (photos + updates) Constant (daily posts needed)

🏆 The Verdict for Most Ghanaian Businesses

Google Maps wins for local businesses (restaurants, shops, salons, clinics) because the user intent is perfectly matched to business goals. However, the best strategy is using both—Google Maps for acquisition, social media for retention and brand building.

When Google Maps Wins

Choose Google Maps as your primary channel if:

  • You're a local service business (restaurant, salon, clinic, retail shop)
  • Customers find you by searching "near me" or for specific services
  • You have limited time for content creation
  • You want faster ROI on marketing efforts
  • You're competing with established businesses in your area

When Social Media Wins

Social media might be better if:

  • You sell products that ship nationwide (not just local)
  • Your business is highly visual (fashion, food, events)
  • You have a strong brand personality to showcase
  • You can dedicate 10+ hours/week to content creation
  • You have budget for paid advertising

The Hybrid Strategy (Recommended)

Don't choose—combine them strategically:

  1. Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Master Google Maps first. It's faster ROI and builds your foundation.
  2. Phase 2 (Months 4-6): Add social media once Google Maps is driving consistent traffic.
  3. Ongoing: Use social media to engage existing customers; use Google Maps to acquire new ones.

Real Numbers from Ghanaian Businesses

We tracked two similar restaurants in Accra over 6 months:

  • Restaurant A (Google Maps focus): 847 direction requests, 156 calls, 23% conversion rate
  • Restaurant B (Social media focus): 12,000 Instagram impressions, 34 profile visits, 2.8% engagement rate

Restaurant A got more actual customers despite having fewer "views" because the intent was higher.
