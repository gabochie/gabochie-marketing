Google Maps Ranking Factors:
What Really Matters in Ghana

Understanding the 10 signals Google uses to decide which Ghanaian businesses appear first on Google Maps.

Published: May 6, 2026 | 8 min read

Every Ghanaian business owner wonders: "Why does my competitor show up first on Google Maps?" The answer lies in understanding Google's ranking algorithm. Here are the factors that actually matter.

The 10 Google Maps Ranking Factors

1Relevance

How well your business category and description match what people are searching for. A restaurant searching "best jollof in Accra" won't show a hardware store, no matter how optimized.

2Distance

Google prioritizes businesses closer to the searcher. You can't control where searchers are, but you CAN ensure your address is accurate and your service areas are properly set.

3Prominence

How well-known your business is online. This includes reviews, backlinks, mentions in local directories, and your overall web presence. Established businesses have an advantage here.

4Review Quantity & Quality

Businesses with more reviews (especially 4+ stars) rank higher. Google trusts businesses that customers trust. A restaurant with 200 reviews beats one with 10 reviews, all else being equal.

5Review Response Rate

Businesses that respond to reviews (both positive and negative) signal to Google that they're active and customer-focused. This is an often-overlooked ranking signal.

6Photo Quantity & Quality

Google favors businesses with photos. Profiles with 10+ photos get significantly more engagement. Photos of your interior, exterior, products, and team all help.

7Google Posts Activity

Weekly posts signal to Google that your business is active. Think of these as mini-updates that keep your profile fresh and relevant.

8Website Quality & Backlinks

If you have a website, its SEO matters. Local backlinks (mentions from Ghana news sites, business directories) boost your Google Maps ranking.

9Q&A Engagement

The Questions & Answers section on your profile is a ranking factor. Answering questions promptly shows Google you're responsive.

10Complete Profile Information

Every empty field is a missed opportunity. Businesses with 100% complete profiles (hours, services, attributes, description) rank higher than incomplete ones.

How These Factors Work Together

Google doesn't use just one factor—it uses all ten in combination. A business with perfect relevance but zero reviews won't outrank a slightly less relevant business with 100 five-star reviews.

The good news? Most Ghanaian businesses only optimize 2-3 of these factors. If you tackle all ten, you'll leapfrog competitors who think "just having a profile" is enough.

The Ghana-Specific Factor

Here's what most international guides miss: mobile optimization. In Ghana, 70%+ of Google Maps searches happen on mobile. If your profile looks bad on a phone (blurry photos, truncated text), Google notices and ranks you lower.

FAQ: Ranking Factor Questions

Does paying for Google Ads help my Maps ranking?

No. Google keeps Google Ads and Google Maps ranking completely separate. Paying for ads won't boost your Maps position, and having a great Maps position won't reduce your ad costs.

How long does it take to improve ranking?

With consistent effort, you'll see initial improvements in 2-4 weeks. Major ranking jumps typically take 2-3 months of sustained optimization activity.

Can a new business outrank an established competitor?

Yes, but it requires superior optimization. Established businesses have "prominence" on their side (factor #3). New businesses must compensate with better completeness (factor #10) and more review velocity (factor #4).

Bottom Line

Google Maps ranking isn't magic—it's a predictable system. Master these 10 factors and you'll outrank competitors who treat Google Maps as an afterthought.

Start with the factors you can control immediately: complete your profile, add photos, start posting weekly, and implement a review generation system. Then tackle the longer-term factors like website backlinks and brand prominence.
