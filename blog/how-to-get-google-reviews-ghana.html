How to Get Google Reviews in Ghana (Step-by-Step Guide)

Google reviews are the single most powerful trust signal for local businesses in Ghana. Here is exactly how to get more of them, manage them, and use them to rank higher on Google Maps.

Gabochie Marketing May 3, 2026 8 min read

Key Stat: Birdeye reports that Google hosts 81% of all online review volume. One extra review can spark about 80 website visits, 63 direction requests, and 16 phone calls on average. In Ghana, where trust drives purchasing decisions, reviews are your most valuable digital asset.

Why Google Reviews Matter for Ghanaian Businesses

Reviews are like online word-of-mouth. In Ghana, where personal trust and recommendation drive most purchasing decisions, Google reviews serve as digital social proof. When a potential customer searches for your service in Accra or Kumasi, they see your star rating and review count before anything else.

Here is why reviews directly impact your business:

  • Google ranks businesses with more positive reviews higher in local search results
  • Recent reviews tell Google your business is still active and serving customers
  • Keywords in reviews (like "best restaurant in Cape Coast") naturally boost your ranking for those searches
  • High ratings build trust with new customers who have never visited your business
  • Review responses show Google and future customers that you are engaged and professional

Step-by-Step: How to Get More Google Reviews

Step 1: Get Your Direct Review Link

Go to your Google Business Profile dashboard and find your "Get more reviews" link. This is a direct link that takes customers straight to the review page. Copy this link and save it. You will use it in every review request.

Step 2: Ask at the Right Moment

The best time to ask for a review is immediately after a positive customer interaction. When a customer says they are happy with your service, that is your moment. Do not wait. Ask them then and there while the positive feeling is fresh.

Step 3: Send the Link via WhatsApp

In Ghana, WhatsApp is the primary communication tool. After a successful transaction, send your customer a polite WhatsApp message with your review link. Keep it short and personal. For example:

"Hi [Name], thank you for choosing [Business Name]. We hope you had a great experience. If you have a moment, we would really appreciate a review on Google. It helps us a lot. Here is the link: [link]. Thank you!"

Step 4: Use a QR Code in Your Physical Location

Print a QR code that links directly to your Google review page. Place it at your checkout counter, on receipts, or on table tents in restaurants. Customers can scan and review in seconds without typing anything.

Step 5: Make It Easy

The easier you make it, the more reviews you will get. Use your direct link. Do not ask customers to search for your business on Google first. Every extra step reduces the likelihood they will complete the review.

Step 6: Respond to Every Review

Respond to every review, good or bad, within 24 hours. This shows Google that your business is active and engaged. It also shows potential customers that you value feedback. For positive reviews, thank the customer. For negative reviews, acknowledge the issue and offer to resolve it professionally.

What Not to Do

  • Do not buy reviews - Google detects fake reviews and will penalize or suspend your profile
  • Do not offer incentives for reviews - Discounts, free products, or money in exchange for reviews violates Google's policies
  • Do not review your own business - Google can detect self-reviews and will remove them
  • Do not ignore negative reviews - Unresponded negative reviews damage your reputation more than the review itself
  • Do not use the same message for every request - Personalize your review requests for better response rates

How Many Reviews Do You Need?

Aim for at least 20-30 positive reviews to start seeing a real SEO boost. However, the quality and recency of reviews matters as much as the total number. A business with 30 reviews posted over the last 6 months will often outrank a competitor with 80 reviews where the last one was over a year ago.

Review recency signals to Google that your business is still active and serving customers. Make getting reviews a consistent, ongoing process, not a one-time campaign.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many Google reviews do I need to rank in Ghana?

Aim for at least 20-30 positive reviews to start seeing a real impact on your Google Maps ranking. However, review recency matters as much as total volume. A business with 30 reviews posted consistently over the past year will often outrank a competitor with 80 reviews where the last one was 14 months ago.

Can bad reviews hurt my Google ranking in Ghana?

Yes, consistently low ratings can negatively impact your ranking. However, replying politely to negative reviews and fixing the underlying issue can turn things around. Google sees responsive businesses as more trustworthy. A 4.3-star rating with 50 reviews is often better than a 5.0 rating with 3 reviews.

Is it okay to ask customers for Google reviews in Ghana?

Yes, it is perfectly acceptable to ask satisfied customers for Google reviews. Google encourages businesses to ask for reviews. Just do not offer incentives (like discounts or free products) in exchange for reviews, as this violates Google's policies and can result in penalties.

Do reviews need to mention keywords for SEO?

It helps but is not required. When customers naturally mention your service and location in their review (e.g., "best tailor in Kumasi"), it reinforces your relevance for those search terms. However, do not ask customers to include specific keywords, as this can be seen as manipulation.

How quickly will new reviews improve my Google Maps ranking?

New reviews can start impacting your ranking within 1-2 weeks. However, significant ranking improvements from a review strategy typically take 1-3 months of consistent review generation and response activity.
